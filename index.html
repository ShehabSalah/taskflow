Philosophy First

Why professionals use TaskFlow instead of other apps

TaskFlow combines context-aware organization with local-first control. You get one focused workspace at a time, plus the full functionality to plan, track, measure, and export your work without tool switching.

Context-Aware by Design

TaskFlow organizes work by real-world context: client, project, task, time, and resources. Switch workspace and instantly see only what belongs to that context.

Privacy & Control

Sensitive files, work logs, and earnings stay on your machine. Local-first, offline-capable, and no mandatory cloud upload.

Fast Finding, Not Hunting

Find the right file, task, or work log faster because everything is already grouped by context. Information is connected, not scattered across tools.

Built for Real Execution

Plan with projects and Kanban tasks, track time and work logs, monitor earnings and overtime, manage resources, and export reports, all in one desktop flow.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about privacy, workspaces, exports, and desktop installs.

TaskFlow is a local-first desktop app for professionals who manage work across clients, employers, or projects. It brings workspaces, projects, Kanban tasks, time tracking, earnings, reports, files, and overtime into one context-aware system on your machine.
A workspace is a separate work context: a client, employer, contract, or personal work area. Each workspace has its own salary settings, projects, tasks, files, time logs, earnings, and reports so your work stays organized and easy to find.
Yes. Create one workspace per client, employer, or contract. When you switch workspaces, TaskFlow shows only that context: projects, tasks, files, timers, earnings, and reports stay separated so nothing gets mixed together.
No. TaskFlow is built to run locally on your desktop. Your workspaces, tasks, time logs, files, and reports stay on your machine. You do not need an active internet connection to track time, manage work, or review earnings.
No. TaskFlow does not require a cloud account and does not upload your work data by default. There is no built-in telemetry or analytics in the core app. Your files, logs, and earnings remain on your computer unless you choose to export or connect an optional integration later.
Yes. You can start a timer at workspace level, link it to a project, or attach it to a specific task. TaskFlow supports all three so you can track time the way your work is actually structured.
Yes. TaskFlow generates reports you can export to Excel and CSV, including timesheets, attendance logs, and overtime summaries. Exports are created from your recorded time logs on your local machine.
The desktop installer guides you through setup. Easy mode can bundle PostgreSQL for a local install with minimal configuration. Advanced mode lets you connect your own PostgreSQL instance if you prefer to manage the database yourself.
TaskFlow is available for Windows 10 and 11 (.exe) and macOS 11 or later on Intel and Apple Silicon Macs (.dmg). Choose the build that matches your device from the download section.
TaskFlow is a local desktop product. You install it on your machine and run your work locally. There is no required cloud account for core features like workspaces, time tracking, tasks, earnings, and reports.
No. Planned roadmap items such as tool integrations, cloud file links, Knowledge Hub, and AI are designed as optional extensions. Local-first remains the foundation: you choose what to connect, and your core work data stays on your machine.
